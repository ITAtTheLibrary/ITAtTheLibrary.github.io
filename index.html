A Poem for Libraries

In hallowed halls of whispered words,
Where tales of old and new converge,
Stands a sanctuary, mightily unfurled,
A portal to every conceivable world.

The library, a cathedral of thought,
Where the wisdom of ages is diligently sought,
Echoes with the rustle of turning page,
A timeless stage, transcending age.

Bound in leather, cloth, and thread,
In every book, a world is spread,
Stories of love, of valor, of dread,
In the reader's heart and mind, they're fed.

Oceans of knowledge in tranquil rows,
Guided by the lantern of those who know,
Where the seeds of curiosity grow,
And the river of enlightenment freely flows.

In the silence, an orchestra plays,
Of words dancing in a ballet of phrase,
The past and future in each display,
In the grand library where time decays.

No palace is as richly stocked,
As libraries, with their endless flock
Of books, where minds are unlocked,
And the doors of perception are gently knocked.

So enter with a humble heart,
Allow the journey to start,
In this place, art and smarts,
Find their ever-glowing hearth.

In the library, great and vast,
A testament to our diverse past,
The world in words, in shadows cast,
A sanctuary built to last.
